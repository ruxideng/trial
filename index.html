Sentinel: Embodied Cooperative Spatial Reasoning and Planning

Xiangye Lin*1, Hongxin Zhang*1, Ruxi Deng2, Qinhong Zhou1, Chuang Gan1
1UMass Amherst, 2University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign,
(* indicates equal contribution)
arXiv Code Twitter

Abstract

In this work, we study the problem of embodied cooperation under dynamic spatial constraints, and build Sentinel Challenge, where multiple decentralized embodied agents must communicate in natural language to agree on a mutually safe and convenient meeting point within large, city-scale outdoor environments. Each agent must then navigate safely while avoiding dynamic sentinels patrolling the area, with a tool providing coarse spatial information. We propose CoSaR, a cooperative spatial reasoning and planning framework that integrates the communicative and planning abilities of foundation models with classical spatial navigation algorithms. Sentinel enables agents to exchange updated situational information, reason about evolving spatial constraints, and collaboratively replan their routes in real time. Across 15 city-level scenes with 3-5 agents, CoSaR consistently leads to faster gathering, shorter path lengths, and improved safety. These gains stem from its ability to jointly leverage dynamic communication, spatial reasoning, and continual adaptation to changing environmental conditions. By formalizing this new setting and providing a scalable benchmark, we aim to build a foundation for advancing cooperative spatial intelligence in embodied multi-agent systems. Code and challenge will be made publicly available.

As illustrated in Fig. 1, embodied agents operate in large-scale, city-level outdoor environments where cooperation must be achieved under dynamic spatial constraints. The left panel presents a global aerial view showing multiple decentralized agents initialized at distant locations, along with dynamically moving sentinels that patrol the environment and create evolving danger zones. Each agent receives only coarse waypoint guidance from the map tool and must refine its route based on local perception and shared information. The top-right panel demonstrates how agents communicate in natural language to negotiate a mutually safe and convenient meeting point, exchanging situational updates such as sentinel sightings and route safety. The bottom-right panel visualizes the egocentric perspective of an agent, highlighting sentinel detection regions and illustrating how agents identify danger zones and refine their navigation plans accordingly. This setting captures the core challenges of cooperative spatial intelligence: agents must integrate perception, communication, and spatial reasoning to dynamically avoid hazards, adapt their routes, and safely gather at a common destination. By combining decentralized decision-making, real-time communication, and spatially grounded planning, Sentinel Challenge provides a realistic and scalable benchmark for evaluating embodied cooperative reasoning in dynamic environments.
